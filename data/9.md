### Drill Entry 58: Feed the Beast
* **Title:** Feed the Beast (Hot Hitter Tempo)
* **Category:** Gameplay
* **Tags:** ["Tempo", "Setting Decisions", "6v6", "Competitive"]
* **durationMinutes:** 25
* **minPlayers:** 12
* **maxPlayers:** 14
* **Purpose:** * Identify the "hot hitter" and maintain offensive momentum.
    * Improve setter decision-making under game-like pressure.
* **Instructions:**
    1. 6v6 format. Coach introduces the ball over the net mimicking a free ball.
    2. Each team plays the ball out until it results in a kill.
    3. The setter is encouraged to "feed" the hitter who is successfully scoring.
    4. Scoring: Player earns individual points for kills or solo blocks. 
    5. The first side to have a single hitter reach 7 kills wins the drill.
* **sourceManual:** "USAV CAP - Ashley Cornett"

### Drill Entry 59: Jump Spin-Power Control
* **Title:** Jump Spin: Power vs. Control
* **Category:** Serve/Receive
* **Tags:** ["Advanced", "Serving", "Jump Spin"]
* **durationMinutes:** 15
* **minPlayers:** 2
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Develop the ability to toggle between a high-velocity power serve and a tactical control spin serve.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Servers stand at the baseline. Targets are set in deep corners (Zones 1 and 5).
    2. Serve 1: Maximum velocity Jump Spin to any location in bounds.
    3. Serve 2: Tactical Jump Spin (higher arc, more spin) to a specific fixed location.
    4. Focus on the toss consistency and the "snap" of the wrist at contact.
* **sourceManual:** "Learn to Compete Matrix (17/18)"

### Drill Entry 60: 2-Person Serve Receive Transition
* **Title:** 2-Person Serve Receive (Elite)
* **Category:** Serve/Receive
* **Tags:** ["Elite", "Serve Receive", "Tactical", "High Intensity"]
* **durationMinutes:** 20
* **minPlayers:** 4
* **maxPlayers:** 8
* **Purpose:** * Challenge primary passers to cover massive court space.
    * Improve lateral movement and decision-making on "seam" balls.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Only 2 passers on the court, covering the entire width of the baseline.
    2. Servers aim specifically for the sideline and the middle seam.
    3. Passers must communicate early to determine who takes the short vs. deep ball.
    4. The goal is 10 perfect passes to the setter target.
* **sourceManual:** "Learn to Compete Matrix (17/18)"

### Drill Entry 61: Solo Block vs. Hitter
* **Title:** Solo Block Timing
* **Category:** Defense
* **Tags:** ["Blocking", "1-on-1", "Timing"]
* **durationMinutes:** 10
* **minPlayers:** 4
* **maxPlayers:** 8
* **Purpose:** * Train blockers to read the attacker's shoulder and time their jump perfectly without help.
* **Instructions:**
    1. One Blocker (B) and one Hitter (H) at the net. One Tosser/Setter.
    2. Setter sets the Hitter.
    3. Blocker must front the hitter, watch the approach, and jump as the hitter's arm begins to swing forward.
    4. Focus on "penetrating" the net (pressing hands over) rather than just jumping high.
* **sourceManual:** "USA Volleyball IMPACT Manual"

### Drill Entry 62: Combined Tandem/X Attack
* **Title:** Combination Attack (X and Tandem)
* **Category:** Attack
* **Tags:** ["Advanced", "Offense", "Combinations", "Middles"]
* **durationMinutes:** 20
* **minPlayers:** 6
* **maxPlayers:** 10
* **Purpose:** * Disrupt the opposing block using crossing patterns.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Middle Attacker runs a "Quick 1" in the center.
    2. Right Side or Outside Hitter crosses behind/over the Middle to hit a gap ball.
    3. Setter must time the release to the crossing hitter as the blockers commit to the middle.
* **sourceManual:** "Train to Train Matrix (15/16)"

### Drill Entry 63: 6-Back vs. 6-Up Defensive Shift
* **Title:** Defensive System Shift: 6-Back to 6-Up
* **Category:** Defense
* **Tags:** ["Systems", "Strategy", "6v6"]
* **durationMinutes:** 15
* **minPlayers:** 12
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Practice transitioning between a deep-court defense (6-Back) and a tip-defense (6-Up) based on the opponent's habits.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Start in 6-Back. Coach initiates a hit.
    2. On a specific signal, the Middle Back (Zone 6) sprints forward to the 10ft line to switch the team into "6-Up" (Man-up) defense.
    3. Players must adjust their perimeter angles immediately.
* **sourceManual:** "Train to Train Matrix (13/14)"
