### Drill Entry 52: Net Ball (Spikezone)
* **Title:** Net Ball - Reaction & Touch
* **Category:** Warm-up
* **Tags:** ["Youth", "Beginner", "Reaction", "Net Play"]
* **durationMinutes:** 10
* **minPlayers:** 4
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Teach young players to react to balls hitting the net.
    * Improve quick hand movement and staying "ready."
* **Instructions:**
    1. Players stand on one side of the net in a semi-circle.
    2. The Coach stands on the other side and throws the ball into the net at different heights and angles.
    3. The first player in line must react to the rebound and "save" the ball by passing it up to themselves or a target.
    4. Progression: Add a second player who must set the "saved" ball.
* **sourceManual:** "Kids Volley Spikezone Guide"

### Drill Entry 53: Crossover Step & Stick
* **Title:** Crossover Step & Stick
* **Category:** Conditioning
* **Tags:** ["Agility", "Plyometric", "Footwork", "Elite"]
* **durationMinutes:** 10
* **minPlayers:** 1
* **maxPlayers:** 15
* **Purpose:** * Improve lateral power and landing stability (essential for blocking and floor defense).
* **Instructions:**
    1. Player stands on the sideline in an athletic stance.
    2. Player performs a powerful crossover step (lead foot crosses over) to the middle of the court.
    3. Land on the outside foot and "stick" the landing for 2 seconds (no wobbling).
    4. Immediately explode back to the starting line.
    5. Perform 3 sets of 8 reps per side.
* **sourceManual:** "CWU Wildcats Training Book"

### Drill Entry 54: Sitting Volleyball (Ground Movement)
* **Title:** Sitting Volleyball - Core & Arms
* **Category:** Gameplay
* **Tags:** ["Inclusion", "Core", "Ball Control", "Fun"]
* **durationMinutes:** 15
* **minPlayers:** 4
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Develop upper body strength and hand-eye coordination without using legs.
    * Focus on core stability and high-contact points.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Lower the net (or use a ribbon). Players must remain seated on the floor at all times.
    2. Play a standard game of 2-v-2 or 3-v-3.
    3. To move, players must use their arms to scoot. 
    4. This emphasizes early hand preparation since they cannot move as quickly as when standing.
* **sourceManual:** "Kids Volley Spikezone Guide"

### Drill Entry 55: The "Bicycle Run" Core Series
* **Title:** Bicycle Run (No Hands)
* **Category:** Conditioning
* **Tags:** ["Core", "Abs", "Endurance"]
* **durationMinutes:** 5
* **minPlayers:** 1
* **maxPlayers:** 20
* **Purpose:** * Build the deep abdominal strength required for "piking" during an attack.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Lie on back with legs extended.
    2. Lift shoulders off the ground, keeping hands behind the head (or hovering at sides for "No Hands" version).
    3. Pedal legs in a cycling motion, bringing opposite elbow to opposite knee.
    4. Maintain a rhythmic pace for 60 seconds.
* **sourceManual:** "CWU Wildcats Training Book"

### Drill Entry 56: Aim for the Hula Hoops
* **Title:** Target Serving - Hula Hoops
* **Category:** Serve/Receive
* **Tags:** ["Youth", "Accuracy", "Serving"]
* **durationMinutes:** 12
* **minPlayers:** 2
* **maxPlayers:** 14
* **Purpose:** * Provide a clear, fun visual target for beginners learning to serve.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Place 4-6 hula hoops in various zones on the court (Corners, center, short).
    2. Players serve from the baseline (or moved up for younger kids).
    3. Aim to land the ball inside a hoop.
    4. Each hoop has a point value based on difficulty (Corners = 5 pts, Center = 1 pt).
* **sourceManual:** "Kids Volley Spikezone Guide"

### Drill Entry 57: 4-Corner Reaction
* **Title:** 4-Corner Reaction Sprint
* **Category:** Conditioning
* **Tags:** ["Reaction", "Sprints", "Agility"]
* **durationMinutes:** 10
* **minPlayers:** 1
* **maxPlayers:** 4
* **Purpose:** * Improve the ability to change direction based on a visual or auditory cue.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Place 4 cones in a square (10ft x 10ft). Player stands in the center.
    2. Cones are numbered 1 through 4.
    3. The Coach calls a number; the player must sprint to that cone, touch it, and return to center.
    4. Variation: Coach uses hand signals instead of voice to train visual "read" skills.
* **sourceManual:** "CWU Wildcats Training Book"
