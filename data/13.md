### Drill Entry 70: Shuttle Ball (Overhead)
* **Title:** Shuttle Ball (Overhead Relay)
* **Category:** Setting
* **Tags:** ["Youth", "Primary", "Movement", "Teamwork"]
* **durationMinutes:** 10
* **minPlayers:** 6
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Improve overhead passing accuracy while moving.
    * Develop the "Set and Move" habit essential for higher-level transition play.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Form two lines (A and B) facing each other across the net (or a line).
    2. Player A1 sets the ball overhand to Player B1.
    3. After the set, Player A1 sprints to the back of Line B.
    4. Player B1 sets the ball back to Player A2 and sprints to the back of Line A.
    5. Keep the shuttle moving as long as possible without the ball hitting the floor.
* **sourceManual:** "Volleyball Lesson Plan FA-1"

### Drill Entry 71: The "Interceptor" Game
* **Title:** The Interceptor (Defensive Vision)
* **Category:** Defense
* **Tags:** ["Youth", "Game-Based", "Reaction", "Communication"]
* **durationMinutes:** 12
* **minPlayers:** 6
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Train players to read ball flight and move into the path of the ball to "intercept" it before it reaches a target.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Place a target (hula hoop or large cone) in the center of the court.
    2. Two "Attackers" stand on the baseline and try to throw/hit the ball into the target.
    3. Three "Interceptors" (Defenders) must guard the target.
    4. Defenders must use a legal volleyball contact (pass or overhead) to deflect the ball away from the target.
    5. Points are awarded for successful deflections.
* **sourceManual:** "Volleyball Lesson Plan FA-1"

### Drill Entry 72: Block and Transition to Hit
* **Title:** Block to Transition (The Opening Move)
* **Category:** Attack
* **Tags:** ["Blocking", "Transition", "Footwork", "Elite"]
* **durationMinutes:** 15
* **minPlayers:** 3
* **maxPlayers:** 9
* **Purpose:** * Master the footwork required to land from a block and immediately become an offensive threat.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Blocker (MB or Pin) starts at the net. 
    2. Perform a maximum block jump.
    3. Upon landing, execute a 45-degree turn and sprint to the 3-meter line (Opening up).
    4. As the player reaches the line, the Coach tosses a ball for an immediate approach and attack.
    5. Emphasize speed and not "watching the ball" while transitioning.
* **sourceManual:** "USA Volleyball Training Diagrams (IMPACT)"

### Drill Entry 73: The "Wipe-Off" Shot (Tooling)
* **Title:** Attacking the Outside Hand (The Wipe)
* **Category:** Attack
* **Tags:** ["Advanced", "Tactical", "Tooling", "Vision"]
* **durationMinutes:** 10
* **minPlayers:** 4
* **maxPlayers:** 8
* **Purpose:** * Teach hitters how to score against a solid block by using the blocker's hands as a tool.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Set up a static blocker on a box, reaching over the net.
    2. The attacker is set a ball that is "tight" (close to the net).
    3. Instead of swinging through the block, the attacker must aim for the outside hand/fingers and "wipe" the ball horizontally toward the antenna.
    4. Goal is for the ball to hit the hands and land out of bounds on the opponent's side.
* **sourceManual:** "USA Volleyball Training Diagrams (IMPACT)"

### Drill Entry 74: Jump Spin Power vs. Control
* **Title:** Jump Spin: Power/Control Toggle
* **Category:** Serve/Receive
* **Tags:** ["Serving", "Advanced", "Jump Spin", "Strategy"]
* **durationMinutes:** 15
* **minPlayers:** 2
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Develop the mental and physical ability to switch between a "killing" serve and a "tactical" serve.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Servers are at the baseline.
    2. Serve 1: Maximum velocity Jump Spin (Power).
    3. Serve 2: High-arc, heavy spin to a specific zone (Control).
    4. If the server misses the Power serve, they MUST make the Control serve or face a "side-out" penalty.
* **sourceManual:** "Learn to Compete Matrix (17/18)"

### Drill Entry 75: 6-Up vs. 6-Back Decision Making
* **Title:** Defensive Read: 6-Up or 6-Back?
* **Category:** Defense
* **Tags:** ["Systems", "6v6", "Tactical", "Communication"]
* **durationMinutes:** 20
* **minPlayers:** 12
* **maxPlayers:** 14
* **Purpose:** * Train the defense to recognize the attacker's intent (Swing vs. Tip) and shift the system in real-time.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Defense starts in a "6-Back" perimeter (Zone 6 is deep).
    2. Coach initiates a ball to an attacker.
    3. If the attacker shows a full arm swing, the defense stays deep.
    4. If the attacker slows their arm and drops their elbow (tipping), the Zone 6 player must sprint forward to "6-Up" (Man-up) to cover the short ball.
* **sourceManual:** "Train to Train Matrix (13/14)"
