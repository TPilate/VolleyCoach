### Drill Entry 64: Shuttle Ball (Overhead)
* **Title:** Shuttle Ball (Overhead Focus)
* **Category:** Setting
* **Tags:** ["Youth", "Primary", "Footwork", "Movement"]
* **durationMinutes:** 10
* **minPlayers:** 4
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Develop movement to the ball and overhead passing (setting) accuracy in a team relay format.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Divide players into two lines facing each other, 10 feet apart.
    2. Player 1 tosses/sets the ball overhand to the first player in the opposite line.
    3. After setting, Player 1 runs to the back of the opposite line.
    4. The receiver sets the ball back and runs to the back of the first line.
    5. Goal is to keep the ball in the air for as many "shuttles" as possible.
* **sourceManual:** "Volleyball Lesson Plan FA-1"

### Drill Entry 65: Interceptor (Passing Accuracy)
* **Title:** Interceptor!
* **Category:** Defense
* **Tags:** ["Youth", "Game-Based", "Passing", "Reaction"]
* **durationMinutes:** 12
* **minPlayers:** 6
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Improve passing accuracy and the ability to "read" the ball trajectory to intercept it before it hits a target.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Place a "target" (a hula hoop or a player) in the middle of the court.
    2. Two "Throwers" try to throw the ball into the target from the sideline.
    3. Three "Interceptors" (Defenders) stand in front of the target and must use a forearm pass to deflect the ball away.
    4. If an interceptor successfully passes the ball to a teammate, they earn a bonus point.
* **sourceManual:** "Volleyball Lesson Plan FA-1"

### Drill Entry 66: Block & Transition to Hit
* **Title:** Block and Transition to Hit
* **Category:** Attack
* **Tags:** ["Blocking", "Transition", "Footwork", "Advanced"]
* **durationMinutes:** 15
* **minPlayers:** 4
* **maxPlayers:** 8
* **Purpose:** * Train the specific footwork of landing from a block and immediately opening up to the court for an approach.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Blocker (B) starts at the net. A Coach (C) is on a box on the opposite side.
    2. (B) jumps for a block. Upon landing, (B) must turn their shoulders to the court and sprint to the 3m line.
    3. As soon as (B) reaches the 3m line, a Setter feeds them a ball for an attack.
    4. Focus on the "Land-Turn-Sprint" sequence without stutter-stepping.
* **sourceManual:** "USA Volleyball Training Diagrams (IMPACT)"

### Drill Entry 67: 3-Person Line vs. 2-Person Receive
* **Title:** Serve Receive Logic: 3 vs 2 Person
* **Category:** Serve/Receive
* **Tags:** ["Tactical", "Strategy", "Serve Receive", "Decision Making"]
* **durationMinutes:** 20
* **minPlayers:** 6
* **maxPlayers:** 12
* **Purpose:** * Test the effectiveness of different reception systems based on the server's ability.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Team A starts in a 3-person line receive. Team B serves 10 balls. Count "3-passes."
    2. Team A shifts to a 2-person elite receive (leaving the setter and OH ready for faster transition). Team B serves 10 balls.
    3. Analyze which system provided a better "Cycle of Action" (Pass -> Set -> Attack).
* **sourceManual:** "Learn to Compete Matrix (17/18)"

### Drill Entry 68: The "Wipe-Off" Shot
* **Title:** Attacking the Outside Hand (The Wipe)
* **Category:** Attack
* **Tags:** ["Hitting", "Tooling", "Smart Play", "Advanced"]
* **durationMinutes:** 10
* **minPlayers:** 4
* **maxPlayers:** 6
* **Purpose:** * Specifically train attackers to "tool" the block by hitting the ball off the outside hand of the blocker so it lands out of bounds.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Place a blocker on a box, holding their hands stiffly over the net.
    2. The attacker is set a ball that is slightly "tight" (close to the net).
    3. Instead of swinging down, the attacker must "wipe" the ball horizontally across the blocker's hands towards the sideline.
* **sourceManual:** "USA Volleyball Training Diagrams (IMPACT)"

### Drill Entry 69: Defensive Read: 6-Up vs. 6-Back
* **Title:** 6-Up vs 6-Back Decision Drill
* **Category:** Defense
* **Tags:** ["Systems", "Tactics", "Decision Making", "6v6"]
* **durationMinutes:** 15
* **minPlayers:** 12
* **maxPlayers:** 14
* **Purpose:** * Train the defense to switch systems mid-rally based on the attacker's body language.
* **Instructions:**
    1. Team starts in "6-Back" (Zone 6 is deep).
    2. Coach tosses a ball to an attacker.
    3. If the attacker shows a full approach, stay in 6-Back.
    4. If the attacker "drops" their elbow or slows their arm (indicating a tip), the Zone 6 player must sprint to "6-Up" (Man-up) to cover the short ball.
* **sourceManual:** "Train to Train Matrix (13/14)"
